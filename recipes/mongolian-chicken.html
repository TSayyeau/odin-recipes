Mongolian Chicken

Description

This dish is like the sister dish to sweet and sour chicken but without the sour tangy flavour. Instead, the sauce is a garlic brown sugar hoisin sauce with a kick from the red chili. It is not overly spicy where you need a tall glass of milk to fan the flames but you can feel that spice at the back of your tongue. A dish to try if you love sweet spicy chicken. If youâ€™re not a fan of spice, feel free to omit the red chili.

Ingredients

  • 6 Chicken Thighs, diced
  • 1/4 cup Cornstarch
  • 1 Stalk Green Onion, finely chopped
  • 1/3 cup Cooking Oil

Sauce

  • 2 tsp Garlic, minced
  • 1 Red Chili, sliced
  • 5 slices Ginger, skin on
  • 4 Tbsp Hoisin Sauce
  • 2 Tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 Tbsp Brown Sugar
  • 2 tsp Sesame Oil
  • 1 Tbsp Cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup Water

Directions

  1. In a small bowl, mix 1 Tbsp cornstarch and 1/2 cup water until well combined. Set aside.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, add chicken thighs followed by cornstarch. Mix until each piece is coated completely. Set aside.
  3. Heat a large pan or wok over medium high heat, adding cooking oil. Carefully add in chicken and fry until crispy, golden brown on all sides. Remove from pan and transfer to a plate. Set aside.
  4. In same pan, add ginger, garlic, and red chili. Fry for 30 seconds.
  5. Next add in hoisin sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, and cornstarch mixed with water. You may need to give the cornstarch water a sstir before adding to pan. Mix ingredients until well combined and sugar is melted.
  6. Toss your chicken back into the pan and coat each piece with sauce. Remove and transfer to serving dish to enjoy.
  7. Garnish with green onion.

Notes

  • For extra sauce, make double the portion of the sauce if you like pairing it with rice.
